Albert Einstein (1879-1955) was a renowned German-born theoretical physicist. His most famous theory is the theory of relativity, which has two parts:

1) Special Theory of Relativity (1905): This theory radically changed the concepts of space, time, energy and matter. Some key points:

- The laws of physics are the same for all non-accelerating observers
- The speed of light in a vacuum is constant, independent of the motion of the light source     
- Time and space are relative concepts, not absolute

2) General Theory of Relativity (1915): This extended the special theory to include gravity and 
accelerated motion. Key points:

- Gravity is not a force, but a consequence of the curvature of space-time caused by the presence of mass/energy
- Light rays bend when passing close to massive objects due to this curvature
- It introduced the famous equation E=mc^2 showing mass and energy are interchangeable

Einstein's theories revolutionized physics and led to developments in nuclear energy, spacecraft technology, and a better understanding of black holes and the universe itself. He is considered one of the most influential scientists of the 20th century.

Here are three easy exercises for beginners to stay fit:

1. Walking: Walking is a simple, low-impact exercise that can be done almost anywhere. Start with a comfortable pace and gradually increase the distance and speed as you become more comfortable. Aim for at least 30 minutes of brisk walking per day.

2. Bodyweight exercises: Exercises that use your own body weight, such as push-ups, squats, and 
lunges, are great for building strength without the need for any equipment. Start with a manageable number of repetitions and gradually increase as you get stronger.

3. Yoga or stretching: Yoga and stretching exercises can help improve flexibility, balance, and 
overall mobility. Look for beginner yoga classes or follow along with online tutorials to learn 
proper form and techniques.

The key for beginners is to start slowly and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts as you become more comfortable. It's also important to listen to your body and rest 
when needed to avoid injury.

Reading books can provide several benefits for mental health:

1. Stress reduction: Reading can be a form of escapism that allows you to take your mind off the stresses and worries of daily life. Getting immersed in a good book can help you relax and unwind.

2. Increased empathy: Reading fiction, in particular, can help you understand different perspectives and develop empathy for others' experiences and emotions.

3. Mental stimulation: Reading challenges your brain, engages your imagination, and keeps your mind active, which can help prevent cognitive decline as you age.

4. Improved focus and concentration: Reading requires focused attention, which can help improve 
your ability to concentrate and stay present in the moment.

5. Vocabulary expansion: Reading exposes you to new words and concepts, which can improve your vocabulary and communication skills.

6. Increased self-awareness: Books can provide insights into human behavior and emotions, helping you better understand yourself and others.

7. Sleep improvement: Reading before bed can help you relax and prepare your mind for sleep, leading to better sleep quality.

8. Mood enhancement: Reading can be a mood-boosting activity, providing a sense of accomplishment, enjoyment, and even humor, depending on the book.

9. Reduced risk of depression: Some studies have suggested that reading can help reduce the risk of developing depression, potentially due to the cognitive and emotional benefits it provides. 

Overall, reading can be a healthy and enjoyable activity that promotes mental well-being by reducing stress, stimulating the mind, and providing opportunities for self-reflection and personal 
growth.
